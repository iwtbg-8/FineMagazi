Why It Matters

  • Console Boost ‚Äì An influx of gamers will buy new consoles to play GTA VI. This phenomenon was very much evident in 2013 when GTA V was released, and it catapulted PS3 and Xbox 360 sales.
  • Subscription Service ‚Äì There will be increased sign-ups for PlayStation Plus and Xbox Game Pass. The online mode for GTA VI should encourage subscriptions for multiplayer gaming.
  • Cross-Sales ‚Äì Once players have purchased consoles, purchases of other games usually follow, increasing revenue-generating capacity.

  • Sony (NYSE: SONY) ‚Äì PlayStation has been the reigning king among all console brands. An increase in hardware sales and subscriptions should be a huge bonus for the revenue.
  • Microsoft (NASDAQ: MSFT) ‚Äì Although the boost may be smaller from the Xbox hardware, the rest of Microsoft's ecosystem would benefit via Game Pass growth and Xbox Live subscriptions.

For traders, GTA VI could be the short-term catalyst for both stocks.

The Trading Strategy "Hype Cycle"

Major entertainment events usually tend to follow a predictable hype cycle in the stock price: Announcement Phase, Pre-Release Anticipation, Release Day Spike, Post-Launch Dip, and Long-Term Stabilization. This means that for the trader, swing trading TTWO and related stocks through these phases could offer easy money.
